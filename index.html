Leak Detection

Recent Job — February 2025

Hidden leak traced and repaired in Putney

📍 Putney, SW15
⏱ 3.5 hours total
🔍 Three previous plumbers had failed to locate it

A slow but persistent leak had been causing rising damp behind the bathroom wall for several weeks. Three other plumbers attended without success. Using acoustic detection and thermal imaging, Dave identified the source in under an hour — a pinhole corrosion in a 15mm copper pipe behind the tile line. Repaired without full wall demolition.

How it was handled
1
Initial assessment
Damp readings taken across the wall with a moisture meter. Pattern mapped using acoustic detection equipment. Area narrowed to a 60cm section.
2
Thermal imaging scan
Leak confirmed behind tile row three, approximately 40cm from the floor. Only the targeted section of tiles removed — no wider demolition needed.
3
Repair carried out
Pinhole corrosion in 15mm copper pipe sealed with push-fit liner joint. Cavity dried. Tiles re-set using waterproof flexible adhesive.
4
Post-repair verification
Zero moisture confirmed behind repaired section. Written damp report provided for insurance purposes. No recurrence reported.
